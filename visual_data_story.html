How We Interact with Ads on Social Media

What is this data about? (fig.1)

This data was collected through self-observation across several social media platforms over a few weeks in March and May 2025. Each row in the dataset represents a moment where I recorded my platform, activity, the number of ads seen, their relevance, and whether people interacted with them.

The goal of this story is to understand how ads are distributed across platforms, how user engagement activities affect ad frequency, and how relevant or interactive these ads appear to be.

Do Entertainment Ads Feel More Relevant? (fig.3)

Reading Relavancy

Reading as a source of entertainment generates a lot of relevance as it covers a large amount of content that can be categorised broadly compared to video type media platforms.

Final Thoughts

From this small-scale observational dataset, a few patterns emerge:

  • YouTube has a lot of ads (fig.1), but not a lot of engagement with the sheer quantity of ad interactions saying they had no interaction

  • Content type influences ad exposure (fig.2) — media consumption tends to favor video type content as they control that media, rather than passively reading and ignoring the ad completely.

  • Relevance impacts engagement — platforms that focus on relevancy (like TikTok and Twitter) also show more ad interaction (fig.1).

  • Most ads are ignored, but a subset are interacted with, especially when they’re aligned with personal interests (fig.3).

  • This shows how even small, personal datasheets can reveal meaningful insights into digital experiences and advertising behaviours.
